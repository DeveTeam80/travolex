Top 10 Places to Visit During Diwali in India and Abroad

17 August

Diwali or the Festival of Lights is celebrated with much pomp and fanfare in India, but its magic is not really bound within the geographical boundaries of the country. Whether you are looking to soak yourself in traditional celebrations or see how people around the world celebrate Diwali, we have a guide that will help you zero in on the perfect destination.

Here are the top 10 places to visit this Diwali, including some international spots.


1. Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, India


  • Why Visit: Varanasi offers a mesmerizing Diwali experience, especially at the ghats of the Ganges where thousands of diyas are floating in the river. Evening Ganga Aarti is a sight to behold, and spiritual energy comes out more with each and every temple and household illuminated with sparkling lights.

2.Jaipur, Rajasthan, India


  • Why Visit: Diwali is celebrated with great pomp and grandeur in the Pink City. Explore bustling markets and experience the royal heritage of the city, solemnly bathed in festival lights. Nahargarh Fort commands a panoramic view of the city, sparkling in Diwali lights, thus being a must-visit.

3.Amritsar, Punjab, India

  • Why Visit: Diwali falls on the same dates as Bandi Chhor Divas in the Golden Temple, and the temple is lit up with flowers and lights; this place is indeed the most surrealisticâ€”spiritually and visually. It is the tranquil surroundings coupled with the reflections of golden lights in the pool lying in front of this magnificent building that create an absolute magic.

4.Kolkata, West Bengal, India

  • Why Visit: Behold the Kali Puja on Diwali night, when the city is all aglow with lights, revering Goddess Kali. This offers a very different kind of cultural experience with the grand pandals and the vibrant cultural programs that make Kolkata great during this festival.

5.Singapore

  • Why Visit: A kaleidoscope of colorful street lights, intricate rangolis, and cultural performances transforms Singapore's Little India during Diwali. The festival brings together the best of tradition mixed with modernity in a cosmopolitan setting. The Deepavali light-up along Serangoon Road is one of the attractive elements that draws both locals and tourists alike.

6.London, United Kingdom


  • Why Visit: Diwali celebrations in London are among the largest outside India. From cultural shows to varieties of food and mesmerizing lights, the events in Trafalgar Square give one a feel of Diwali in a world city. The multicultural celebration is also coupled with music and dance programs hence vibrant in nature.

7.Udaipur, Rajasthan, India

  • Why Visit: The City of Lakes offers a royal experience in Diwali with its lovely palaces and lakes liveliest with decorations and illuminations, hence ideal for a secluded and romantic holiday. Adding to the charm are the traditional aarti at Jagdish Temple and fireworks over Lake Pichola.

8.Mysore, Karnataka, India


  • Why Visit: The glamor of Mysore Palace with Diwali illuminations is incomparable as this attraction is combined with the rich culture of the city. Many of the annual Dasara festivities continue to embrace Diwali and offer a unique blend of rich cultural events and celebrations.

9.Dubai, United Arab Emirates


  • Why Visit: The festivities of Diwali are absolutely stunning in Dubai, be it the fireworks, light shows, or cultural activities across the city. Shopping festivals accompanied by special events on Diwali create an ultra-luxury getaway. One of the special Diwali shows at Dubai Festival City Mall and the fireworks display at Burj Khalifa become part of the limelight of the celebration.

10.Fiji


  • Why Visit: With its large Indian population, Fiji celebrates Diwali with immense enthusiasm. This island country beautifies the traditional Diwali celebrations with the essence of the tropical paradise. Communities at a local level can be seen rejoicing with cultural programs, fireworks, and meals of celebration.

Conclusion:
