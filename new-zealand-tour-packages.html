  • Day 1

    Arrival in Auckland

    Upon arrival at Auckland International Airport, guests will be warmly greeted by a Travolex representative and transferred to their hotel in the heart of the city. After check-in, the day is free for guests to rest or explore New Zealand’s largest city at their own pace. They may take a stroll around the Viaduct Harbour or enjoy the views from the Sky Tower. Overnight stay will be in Auckland.

  • Day 2

    Auckland City Tour – Rotorua

    After breakfast, guests begin the day with a panoramic city tour of Auckland, including sights such as Queen Street, the Auckland Domain, Harbour Bridge, and Mission Bay. Following the city tour, they will travel by road to Rotorua, a geothermal wonderland and center of Maori culture. En route, a stop will be made at the Waitomo Glowworm Caves, where guests enjoy a guided boat ride through the caves illuminated by thousands of tiny glowworms. Upon arrival in Rotorua, guests check in to their hotel and relax for the evening. Overnight stay will be in Rotorua.
  • Day 3

    Rotorua Sightseeing

    The day begins with a visit to Te Puia, a geothermal reserve home to the famous Pohutu Geyser, bubbling mud pools, and hot springs. Guests will also witness traditional Maori carving and weaving, and learn about the cultural heritage of the Maori people. The tour continues with a visit to Rainbow Springs Nature Park, where native wildlife, including the iconic Kiwi bird, can be seen. Later, guests enjoy the Agrodome Sheep Show, a fun and interactive experience showcasing New Zealand’s sheep farming industry. In the evening, guests may opt for a traditional Maori cultural performance with a Hangi dinner (optional). Overnight stay will be in Rotorua.
  • Day 4

    Rotorua – Queenstown (Flight)

    After breakfast, guests are transferred to the Rotorua Airport for a domestic flight to Queenstown, the adventure capital of New Zealand. Upon arrival, they are taken to their hotel with the rest of the day free for leisure. Guests may explore the picturesque town center, relax by Lake Wakatipu, or take the Skyline Gondola for panoramic views of Queenstown and the surrounding mountains. Overnight stay will be in Queenstown.
  • Day 5

    Milford Sound Cruise

    This day is dedicated to one of New Zealand’s most breathtaking natural attractions – Milford Sound. Guests embark on a scenic drive through the Fiordland National Park, passing Te Anau, Eglinton Valley, and the Homer Tunnel. Upon arrival, they board a cruise to explore the majestic fjord, surrounded by sheer cliffs, waterfalls, and Mitre Peak rising dramatically from the waters. Lunch is served onboard as the cruise glides through the pristine landscape. After the cruise, guests return to Queenstown in the evening. Overnight stay will be in Queenstown.
  • Day 6

    Queenstown – Day at Leisure / Optional Activities

    After breakfast, the day is free for guests to explore Queenstown at their own pace. For adventure enthusiasts, optional activities include bungee jumping, jet boating, skydiving, or paragliding. Guests preferring a more relaxed day may take a wine tour, enjoy a lake cruise on the TSS Earnslaw, or visit Arrowtown, a charming gold rush village. In the evening, Queenstown’s lively restaurants and bars offer plenty of options for dining and entertainment. Overnight stay will be in Queenstown.
  • Day 7

    Queenstown – Auckland (Flight)

    After breakfast, guests check out and are transferred to the airport for a flight back to Auckland. Upon arrival, they are transferred to their hotel for check-in. The rest of the day is free for final sightseeing or shopping. Guests may choose to visit the Auckland Art Gallery, take a sunset harbour cruise, or enjoy a farewell dinner overlooking the city skyline. Overnight stay will be in Auckland.
  • Day 8

    Departure from Auckland

    After breakfast and depending on the flight schedule, guests will be transferred to Auckland International Airport for their departure flight. With unforgettable memories of stunning landscapes, vibrant cities, and warm Kiwi hospitality, Travolex bids a fond farewell as the New Zealand adventure comes to an end.

Frequently Asked Questions

The ideal time to visit New Zealand depends on the kind of experience you're looking for. Summer, from December to February, is perfect for outdoor adventures, beach trips, and festivals. If you’re interested in snow sports like skiing or snowboarding, winter from June to August is the best time to visit the South Island. For those who prefer mild weather and fewer crowds, spring (September to November) and autumn (March to May) offer a pleasant climate and beautiful natural landscapes.


Yes, Indian passport holders are required to obtain a visitor visa before traveling to New Zealand. The application can be submitted online through the official New Zealand Immigration website. The processing time usually ranges from 20 to 25 working days. Many travel agencies, including Travolex, provide visa assistance services as part of their package offerings to make the process smoother for travelers.


Most New Zealand tour packages include iconic destinations such as Auckland, known for its vibrant city life; Rotorua, famous for its geothermal activity and rich Maori culture; and Queenstown, the adventure capital of the world. Other commonly included spots are Milford Sound for its stunning fjords, Lake Tekapo for its scenic beauty and stargazing, and the charming cities of Wellington and Christchurch for cultural experiences. Tour packages can often be customized based on individual preferences, whether you're looking for adventure, nature, or luxury experiences.
