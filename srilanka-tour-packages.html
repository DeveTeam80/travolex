  • Day 1

    Arrival in Colombo – Transfer to Kandy

    Upon arrival at Bandaranaike International Airport, guests will be warmly welcomed by a representative from Travolex. After a brief introduction and assistance with luggage, the journey begins with a scenic drive towards Kandy, the cultural capital of Sri Lanka. On the way, there will be a stop at the Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage, where guests can observe rescued elephants in a semi-natural habitat. Upon reaching Kandy, guests will check in at their hotel and have some time to unwind. In the evening, they will visit the sacred Temple of the Tooth Relic, one of the most revered Buddhist sites in the country. Later, they will witness a traditional Kandyan cultural dance performance before retiring for the night in Kandy.

  • Day 2

    Kandy to Nuwara Eliya

    After breakfast, the tour continues with a visit to the lush Royal Botanical Garden in Peradeniya, known for its extensive collection of orchids and tropical flora. From there, the journey moves upward into the misty hills towards Nuwara Eliya, affectionately known as “Little England” for its colonial charm and cool climate. Along the way, guests will visit a tea factory and plantation, learning about Sri Lanka’s famous Ceylon tea. They will also make a short stop at the picturesque Ramboda Falls. Upon reaching Nuwara Eliya, guests will enjoy a guided city tour including visits to Gregory Lake, Victoria Park, and the iconic red-bricked post office. The evening is free to relax or explore the charming town before spending the night in Nuwara Eliya.
  • Day 3

    Nuwara Eliya – Ella – Yala

    The day begins early with breakfast followed by a scenic train journey from Nanu Oya to Ella, one of the most beautiful train rides in the world. Upon arrival in Ella, the group will explore highlights such as the Ella Gap, Ravana Falls, and the famous Nine Arches Bridge, a marvel of colonial-era engineering. Later in the day, the journey continues to Yala, located in the southeastern region of Sri Lanka. After checking in at a hotel near Yala National Park, guests will have time to relax and prepare for the next day’s early morning safari. Overnight stay will be in Yala.
  • Day 4

    Yala Safari – Bentota

    Guests will start the day early with an exciting jeep safari in Yala National Park, renowned for its population of leopards, elephants, and a variety of bird species. After the safari and breakfast at the hotel, the journey continues toward the coastal town of Bentota. This drive offers scenic views of the countryside and the southern coastline. Upon arrival in Bentota, guests will check in at a beachfront resort and enjoy a relaxing evening by the Indian Ocean. The remainder of the day is at leisure. Overnight stay will be in Bentota.
  • Day 5

    Bentota – Colombo

    After breakfast, guests will enjoy a boat safari along the Madu River, gliding past mangrove forests, small islands, and traditional fishing villages. The tour includes a visit to a sea turtle conservation hatchery, where guests can learn about the protection and release of endangered sea turtles. Optional water sports activities such as jet skiing or banana boating are available for the more adventurous. In the afternoon, the group will proceed to Colombo, Sri Lanka’s bustling commercial capital. A city tour will follow, covering landmarks such as Galle Face Green, the Gangaramaya Temple, Independence Square, and a visit to popular shopping venues like ODEL or House of Fashion. Guests will spend their final night in Colombo.
  • Day 6

    Departure from Colombo

    After breakfast and depending on the flight schedule, guests will have some free time for last-minute shopping or relaxation. The Travolex team will then provide a comfortable transfer to Bandaranaike International Airport for departure, marking the end of an unforgettable journey through the heart of Sri Lanka.
