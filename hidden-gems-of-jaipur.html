1. Galtaji Temple - The Monkey Temple


Nestled in the Aravalli hills, Galtaji Temple, also known as the Monkey Temple, is a hidden spiritual haven. Unlike typical temples, Galtaji comprises several sacred kunds (water tanks) where pilgrims bathe and monkeys roam freely, adding a touch of wild beauty. With its intricate carvings, pavilions, and natural springs, it’s a great place for visitors to experience Jaipur’s spiritual essence away from the city’s hustle. Travolex can help arrange transportation and guided tours, ensuring you fully experience this remarkable place.



2. Panna Meena Ka Kund - The Ancient Stepwell


Panna Meena Ka Kund is an 8th-century step well known for its symmetrical stairways and captivating geometry. Located near Amber Fort, this spot is perfect for architecture and photography enthusiasts. The stepwell not only served as a water reservoir but also as a communal spot for locals, making it a fascinating place to witness Jaipur’s historical water conservation practices. With Travolex, you can get access to expert guides who share the story and architecture behind this engineering marvel



3. Anokhi Museum of Hand Printing


Situated in a restored haveli near Amber Fort, the Anokhi Museum of Hand Printing is a tribute to Jaipur’s traditional block-printing artistry. The museum offers workshops, exhibits on techniques, and showcases a collection of handcrafted textiles, preserving an age-old craft integral to Rajasthani culture. Travolex arranges special tours where travellers can try their hand at block-printing, gaining an immersive experience in this unique art form.



4. Chand Baori - Stepwell in Abhaneri Village

About an hour’s drive from Jaipur, Chand Baori in Abhaneri village is one of the largest and oldest stepwells in Rajasthan. With over 3,500 narrow steps descending 13 stories, it’s a striking example of ancient water harvesting and engineering techniques. Though slightly off the beaten path, Travolex can organise day trips to Chand Baori, ensuring visitors explore this architectural masterpiece and understand its cultural significance.



5. Jawahar Kala Kendra - Art and Cultural Hub

A modern cultural complex inspired by Jaipur’s traditional Vastu Shastra, Jawahar Kala Kendra (JKK) is a lesser-known gem. With galleries, theatres, and cafes, it’s a dynamic space that promotes local artists and cultural events. JKK hosts various performances, art exhibitions, and workshops, making it a vibrant cultural experience for visitors looking to dive into Jaipur’s modern artistic expressions. Travolex helps visitors stay updated with JKK’s calendar, offering a chance to experience Jaipur’s evolving art scene.



6. Nahargarh Biological Park


This 720-hectare park near Nahargarh Fort offers a refreshing break from the typical fort tours. The park’s safari experience allows visitors to see leopards, hyenas, sloth bears, and more, as well as the rich flora unique to the Aravallis. The biodiversity and serenity of this park offer a more intimate experience with Jaipur’s natural heritage. Travolex provides guided nature walks and safaris, perfect for wildlife enthusiasts seeking an eco-friendly adventure.



7. Hathni Kund - Nature’s Hidden Gem


For those who love trekking, Hathni Kund, located just a short drive from Jaipur, is a scenic spot surrounded by lush greenery, waterfalls, and natural pools. A lesser-known destination among tourists, it’s an ideal spot for adventure seekers who want to experience Rajasthan’s natural beauty. Travolex can arrange day trips, complete with experienced trekking guides, allowing travellers to enjoy a safe and unforgettable journey into Jaipur’s wilderness.



8. Sanganer - Hub of Handmade Paper and Blue Pottery


The town of Sanganer, located on the outskirts of Jaipur, is famous for its hand-made paper and exquisite blue pottery. The artisans here have preserved traditional techniques, and the town’s workshops offer a peek into Jaipur’s artisanal heritage. With Travolex as your travel partner, you can embark on a tour of these artisan workshops, gaining insight into the meticulous processes that go into each craft.



9. Sisodia Rani Garden and Palace


Built as a symbol of love, the Sisodia Rani Garden and Palace is a landscaped paradise with tiered gardens, murals, and pavilions. Inspired by Mughal and Rajput designs, the gardens showcase stories from Indian mythology, making it a tranquil escape. Located slightly off the main tourist trail, Travolex provides visitors with private tours and transportation to ensure a peaceful, unhurried experience.
