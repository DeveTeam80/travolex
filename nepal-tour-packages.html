  • Day 1

    Arrival in Kathmandu

    Upon arrival at Tribhuvan International Airport in Kathmandu, a Travolex representative will greet you and assist with your transfer to the hotel. After check-in, enjoy some leisure time to rest or explore the lively streets of Thamel, known for its handicraft shops, trekking gear stores, and charming cafés. In the evening, join a traditional Nepali welcome dinner with cultural dance performances to begin your journey with a taste of local hospitality and flavor.
  • Day 2

    Kathmandu Valley Sightseeing

    After breakfast, embark on a full-day exploration of the Kathmandu Valley’s UNESCO World Heritage Sites. Begin with a visit to Swayambhunath Stupa (Monkey Temple), perched atop a hill with panoramic views of the city. Then proceed to the sacred Pashupatinath Temple, the most revered Hindu temple in Nepal, located along the Bagmati River. Continue to the serene Boudhanath Stupa, a major pilgrimage site for Tibetan Buddhists. In the afternoon, explore Patan Durbar Square, a living museum of ancient palaces, courtyards, and temples showcasing exquisite Newari architecture. Return to your hotel in the evening with time to relax or enjoy dinner at a rooftop restaurant.
  • Day 3

    Drive or Fly to Pokhara

    After breakfast, travel to Pokhara, Nepal’s scenic lake city nestled against the backdrop of the Annapurna range. The drive takes about 6–7 hours through picturesque hills and river valleys, with stops for tea or photos along the way. Alternatively, a short 25-minute flight can be arranged for a quicker and more comfortable journey. Upon arrival, check in to your hotel and spend the evening by the tranquil Phewa Lake, where you can enjoy a boat ride or simply unwind at one of the lakeside cafés as the sun sets behind the mountains.
  • Day 4

    Pokhara Sightseeing and Himalayan Views

    Rise early for a sunrise drive to Sarangkot, a viewpoint offering breathtaking views of the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri ranges, including the iconic Machapuchare (Fishtail Peak). Return to the hotel for breakfast, then set out for a day of local sightseeing. Visit the mysterious Gupteshwor Cave, the sacred Devi’s Fall, and the International Mountain Museum, which chronicles the history of mountaineering in Nepal. Afterward, enjoy some leisure time for shopping or optional activities like paragliding or cycling. Your evening is free to explore the lakeside promenade, with optional live music and dining at local restaurants.
  • Day 5

    Return to Kathmandu – Free Evening

    After breakfast, return to Kathmandu by road or flight, depending on your travel preference. Upon arrival, check in to your hotel and enjoy your final evening in Nepal at your own pace. You may choose to explore Bhaktapur Durbar Square, known for its well-preserved medieval architecture, or simply enjoy a relaxed dinner with a view of the Kathmandu skyline. For those interested, an optional spiritual session with a local monk or a yoga class can be arranged to reflect on your journey.
  • Day 6

    Departure from Kathmandu

    After breakfast, check out from your hotel and transfer to Tribhuvan International Airport for your onward journey. Depart with beautiful memories of Nepal’s rich culture, warm people, and the towering beauty of the Himalayas.

Frequently Asked Questions

Yes, Nepal is generally safe for tourists. The country is known for its hospitality and welcoming culture. Travolex ensures all tours are operated with experienced guides and verified transport and accommodation partners to prioritize your safety.


Most Travolex Nepal packages focus on cities like Kathmandu (1,400m) and Pokhara (800m), which are at low altitudes and pose no risk of altitude sickness. Altitude-related risks only apply to high-altitude treks like Everest Base Camp, and we take precautions if such activities are included.


Nepal experiences four distinct seasons, with spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) being the most popular times to visit. Spring offers warm days and vibrant rhododendron blooms across the hills, while autumn provides clear skies and the most breathtaking mountain views. Monsoon season (June to August) brings heavy rainfall, especially in hilly and rural areas, which can affect travel plans. Winter (December to February) is chilly but generally dry, especially in the Kathmandu and Pokhara regions, making it suitable for sightseeing with lighter tourist crowds.
