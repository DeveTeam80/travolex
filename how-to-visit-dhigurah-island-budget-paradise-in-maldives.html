Why Choose Dhigurah Island?


Dhigurah, meaning "long island" in Dhivehi, lives up to its name, with its stretch of powdery white sand and lush vegetation spanning over three kilometers. Located in the Alif Dhaal Atoll, Dhigurah offers an authentic Maldivian experience. Unlike resort islands, this inhabited island provides travelers a chance to interact with locals, explore a serene environment, and enjoy water activities at a fraction of the cost.


How to Reach Dhigurah Island

Reaching Dhigurah Island is easier than you might think, thanks to its accessibility:

  1. Fly to Malé International Airport (Velana International Airport)
    Your journey begins with a flight to Malé, the capital of the Maldives. Many airlines offer direct flights from major international cities to Malé.

  2. Domestic Flight or Speedboat to Dhigurah

    • Domestic Flight:From Malé, take a short domestic flight to Maamigili Airport. The flight typically takes 20 minutes. From there, a speedboat ride to Dhigurah will have you on the island in just 10-15 minutes.

    • Speedboat Transfer:For budget travelers, speedboats operate directly from Malé to Dhigurah, with the journey taking around 2-3 hours.

Top Activities to Enjoy on Dhigurah Island



  1. 1. Snorkeling and Diving

    Dhigurah is a diver’s paradise, surrounded by vibrant coral reefs teeming with marine life. It’s one of the best places to spot whale sharks, manta rays, and colorful reef fish. Dive centers on the island offer affordable packages for beginners and certified divers alike.

  2. 2. Relax on the Bikini Beach

    Unlike many local islands, Dhigurah has a designated bikini beach where tourists can sunbathe and swim freely. This secluded stretch of sand is perfect for unwinding with a good book or soaking up the tropical sun.

  3. 3. Whale Shark Excursions

    Dhigurah is renowned as a hotspot for whale shark sightings, thanks to its location near a protected marine area. Join a snorkeling excursion for a chance to swim alongside these gentle giants, an experience you'll never forget.

  4. 4. Explore the Sandbank

    At the southern tip of Dhigurah, a breathtaking sandbank stretches into the azure waters. Accessible by boat or a short walk during low tide, this idyllic spot is perfect for picnics, sunset views, or photography.

  5. 5. Fishing Trips

    Experience traditional Maldivian fishing by joining a fishing excursion. Night fishing is particularly popular, offering a chance to catch reef fish and enjoy a fresh seafood barbecue.

  6. 6. Island Hopping

    Nearby islands like Rangali or Maamigili can be explored through day trips. These excursions provide insight into the diverse culture and beauty of the Maldives.

  7. 7. Cultural Experiences

    Dhigurah allows visitors to immerse themselves in local culture. Interact with the friendly islanders, try traditional Maldivian cuisine, and witness local crafts.

Dining Options on Dhigurah Island

The guesthouses on Dhigurah often include meals in their packages, serving a mix of Maldivian and international cuisines. If you’re looking to explore local flavors, visit one of the small eateries on the island.

  • Local Dishes to Try:

    • Garudhiya:A traditional fish soup served with rice, lime, and chilies.

    • Mas Huni:A breakfast favorite made of tuna, coconut, and onions, served with flatbread.

    • Fihunu Mas:Grilled fish marinated with Maldivian spices.

    Dining on Dhigurah is not only delicious but also incredibly affordable.

Budget Tips for Visiting Dhigurah Island



  1. 1. Book Early

    Secure the best rates for flights, accommodations, and transfers by booking well in advance.


  2. 2. Choose Package Deals

    Guesthouses often provide packages that include meals, activities, and excursions, saving you money and time.

  3. 3. Travel During the Off-Season

    The Maldives' off-season (May to October) offers lower rates on accommodations and activities.

  4. 4. Use Public Speedboats

    Opt for public speedboat transfers instead of private ones to cut down on transportation costs.

  5. 5. Bring Essentials

    Since the island has limited shopping options, bring essentials like sunscreen, toiletries, and snorkeling gear to avoid extra expenses.

Sustainable Travel Practices on Dhigurah

As Dhigurah thrives on eco-tourism, travelers are encouraged to:

  • Minimize plastic waste by carrying reusable bottles and bags.

  • Respect local customs and dress modestly outside the bikini beach.

  • Support local businesses by dining and shopping on the island.

  • Follow guidelines while snorkeling or diving to protect the coral reefs.
