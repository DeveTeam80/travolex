Top Experiences in London

1. Iconic Landmarks

Start your journey with a visit to Big Ben, the Houses of Parliament, and Westminster Abbey. From there, take a stroll to Buckingham Palace and witness the Changing of the Guard.

2. The Tower of London & Tower Bridge

Dive into London’s rich history with a tour of the Tower of London, home to the Crown Jewels. Walk across the stunning Tower Bridge and snap some of the best city photos.

3. The London Eye & Thames Cruise

View the skyline aboard the London Eye and enjoy a scenic River Thames cruise—a relaxing way to see the city from a different angle.

4. Museums & Markets

Visit the British Museum, Tate Modern, and National Gallery for free. Then shop and eat your way through Borough Market, Camden, or Portobello Road.

5. West End Shows

Experience London’s theater scene with a musical or play in the West End—from Les Misérables to The Phantom of the Opera.

When to Visit London

The

best time to visit London

depends on your travel goals, but spring (March to May) is often considered ideal thanks to mild weather, blooming gardens, and fewer crowds. Summer (June to August) brings vibrant festivals, longer days, and outdoor events, though it’s also the busiest and most expensive season. For those looking to avoid the crowds while still enjoying pleasant weather, autumn (September to October) offers crisp air and colorful parks. Meanwhile, winter (November to February) is quieter and more affordable, with festive Christmas markets and cozy pubs—just be prepared for chilly, often rainy days.
