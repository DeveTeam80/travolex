1. Mekong Delta Cruise :

The Mekong Delta paints an exquisite picture of the country’s rustic and attractive rural countryside and is undoubtedly one of Vietnam’s most alluring tourist destinations. One of the most popular and nicest things to do in South Vietnam is to cruise down this picturesque deltaic watercourse. Sail aboard a traditional Vietnamese boat in the Mekong Delta is a perfect delight for visitors.

2. Halong Bay Kayaking and Cruising :

The Halong Bay, often called the ‘descending dragons,’ is dotted with lush green islands. Apart from taking in the wonderful beauty of the location, some of the exciting activities to do in Vietnam with friends include cruising in antique Chinese junk and kayaking in the bay area. The cruise fee is around VND 2,700,000. Charges vary depending on the type of cruise, services provided, and length of stay.

3. Exploring The Cu Chi Tunnel Network :

Cu Chi is famous for its remarkable subterranean tunnel network, constructed during the American and French War in 1940. This 250-kilometer-long tunnel web was built for communication purposes, including locked doors and hidden passages allowing a safe escape to surrounding streams, rivers, and canals. Many of the tunnels have been expanded and made accessible to tourists. People can crawl through them and get a sense of how residents used to hide in such a stifling environment.

4. Feast On Snake, Pho and Scorpion :

Pampering the taste buds is one of the nicest things to do in Vietnam, as it is in any other place. And the experience is made even better by the delectable Pho and scalding hot Vietnamese coffee. Pho, a traditional Vietnamese cuisine, consists of broth, rice noodles, veggies, herbs, and meat. Order Pho at one of Vietnam’s well-known restaurants to get the most authentic and exquisite taste. Among these are Pho 2000, Pho 24, Pho Bo Vien Thap Cam, Pho Quynh, Pho Hoa, and Pho Thin Ha Noi. Local coffee should not be missed at M2C Café, Cosmo Café, L’usine Dong Khoi, and Thoai Vien Café. Scorpion Rice Wine and Snake Wine (made from Cobra) are must-try cocktails for individuals who enjoy experimenting with food. The drinks are not only edible and harmless but also extremely good for one’s health.

5. Musical Spectacular At The Saigon Opera House :

If you enjoy music, include an opera performance at the Saigon Opera House in your Vietnam travel itinerary. This former assembly house of South Vietnam was built in 1897. The structure is beautifully designed in the French Colonial style. Visiting the Saigon Opera House and seeing a show by the Ho Chi Minh City Ballet Symphony Orchestra and Opera is undoubtedly one of the top things to do in Vietnam. There is no admission fee. However, tickets for the specific show must be purchased. Ticket costs vary according to the show, time, and location.

6. Living Alongside The Historical Displays Of Ha L Prison :

This heritage monument, ironically called Hall Prison, is where political prisoners were imprisoned during the American War. This is one of Vietnam’s most popular tourist attractions, also known as the Hanoi Hilton. Visitors to Hilton Prison come to witness many objects linked to the Vietnamese struggle during various wars. Photographers and history buffs will like the prison-turned-museum.

7. Taking In Ho Chi Minh City’s Vibrant Nightlife :

The nightlife culture in Ho Chi Minh City is defined by loud party music, sizzling pubs, DJs, bands, and exquisite food. From posh pubs to affordable bars, the city caters to people of all tastes and budgets. Among the enjoyable things to do in Ho Chi Minh City is to relax with wine and great music. District 1 offers elegant rooftop pubs and bars that sell the greatest wine and champagne and play fantastic music. Pham Ngu Lao Street features many street-side pubs with inexpensive beer and food, live music, and a relaxed atmosphere

8. Buy The Best Silk Products At Hoi An :

Picturesque Hoi An is well-known around the world for its silk weavers and skilled tailors. Visitors to this coastal village can go shopping for silk products such as gowns, fabric, and scarves. Numerous small shops litter the narrow passageways and busy streets of Hoi An. The retail environment is controlled by very brilliant tailors who are pros at stitching customized apparel, shirts, jackets, and shoes at amazingly low prices

9. Hiking And Cycling In Mai Chau :

Mai Chau is a picturesque countryside located distant from the hustle and bustle of Hanoi. People come from all over the world to enjoy the verdant peaks, rolling rice fields, traditional thatched roof cottages, winding paths, and tranquility of the environment. Hiking up to the hills for a spectacular view and cycling around the hamlet are two of the most exciting activities in Vietnam. Plan a stay in one of the home stays here.

10. Shopping Until You Drop :

Visitors to Vietnam can go mad buying silk cloth, cosmetics, souvenirs, pearls, handicrafts, terracotta & ceramic objects, and spices. Shopping for these things at various tourist spots is one of the finest ways to spend a week in Vietnam.

How To Reach Vietnam?



By Sea



Have you ever wondered what it would be like to travel in the manner of our forefathers? Nothing compares to the pure emptiness of relaxing aboard a beautiful ship, with every need met. While no direct cruises are available from India, there are various possibilities from Singapore. The cruise from Ho Chi Minh City to Singapore takes approximately 9-10 days.

By Air



The distance between Vietnam and India is around 3,194 kilometers, and your flight time will be approximately 4 hours. With over 300 flights every week connecting the two countries, flying to Vietnam from Bangalore is the most convenient option. You can, however, fly from other important cities including Cochin, Delhi, Amritsar, Hyderabad and Thiruvananthapuram. Vietnam has three international airports, and you can choose your port of arrival based on which section of the country most interests you. You can fly into Hanoi’s Noi Bai International Airport in the north, Ho Chi Minh City’s Tan Son Nhat International Airport in the south, or Da Nang International Airport in the center. The airport in Hanoi is around 40 minutes from the city center, whereas the airports in Ho Chi Minh City and Da Nang are 20 and 10 minutes. Although the airports are minor in comparison to other international airports, they all provide basic services like money exchange shops, food and ATMs. The advantage of smaller airports is that it becomes easier to navigate without getting lost or missing your connection flight. Vietnam also has strong domestic connectivity, and you may fly to any area of the nation from any of these airports.

Wrapping Up

However, due to the country’s natural and ecological diversity, it is difficult to recommend a specific month or season to visit Vietnam. As a result, the optimum time to visit differs according to the many places to visit in Vietnam.
