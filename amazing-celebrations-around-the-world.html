1. Carnival – Rio de Janeiro, Brazil


There's nothing like Carnival in Rio! This dynamic and exciting event is famous for its grandiose parades, samba music, and eye-catching costumes. 2. Oktoberfest – Munich, Germany


Oktoberfest is the world’s largest beer festival, attracting millions of visitors to Munich every year. Running from late September to early October, this festival celebrates Bavarian culture with traditional folk music, hearty German food, and of course, plenty of beer! At Travolex, we tailor the

3. Diwali – India


Diwali is termed the Festival of Lights. It is a festival done with much excitement and fun in almost all areas of India. The festival spans five days in which houses and streets are decorated with oil lamps and colorful decorations and lit up by fireworks at night. It marks the conquest of light over darkness and good over evil. Diwali celebration and cultural richness: Travel to Varanasi and Jaipur in the tour packages offered by Travolex to see the magic of this iconic festival.



4. Chinese New Year – China

Chinese New Year also known as Lunar New Year, is celebrated with magnificent events throughout China and other parts of Asia. This event, which includes traditional lion and dragon dances, fireworks, and crimson decorations, brings prosperity and good fortune for the coming year. Our

customized China tour packages

allow you to visit locations such as Beijing and Hong Kong to enjoy the colorful and energetic atmosphere of Chinese New Year.


5. Holi – India

Holi, or the festival of colors, is one of the most exciting and joyful celebrations in the world. To celebrate the arrival of spring, people fling colored powders at one another in an enthusiastic and lively manner. The streets are alive with laughter, music, and dance, making Holi a festival that you must attend. Travolex provides India vacation packages that take you to towns such as Mathura and Vrindavan, where Holi is celebrated with great enthusiasm.



6. La Tomatina – Buñol, Spain

Plan to paint the town red during La Tomatina , one of the world's most distinctive events. La Tomatina, held in Buñol, Spain, is a large tomato fight involving thousands of participants throwing tomatoes at one another. If you're looking for a unique and adventurous festival experience, Travolex's

7. Day of the Dead – Mexico

Día de los Muertos , often known as the Day of the Dead, is a popular Mexican ritual that commemorates deceased loved ones. This festival, which includes colorful altars, painted skulls, and street parades, combines remembrance with celebration. Travolex allows you to discover Mexico during this sacred time while also participating in the rich cultural traditions that make this festival so distinct.



8. St. Patrick’s Day – Ireland

St. Patrick's Day is celebrated around the world, but it is nowhere more authentic than in Ireland itself. The festival, commemorating Ireland's patron saint, parades, music, and champagne, ensures you share a perfectly fun-filled festival of Dublin, Ireland with our travel packages for Ireland.



9. Songkran – Thailand

Songkran, or Thai New Year, is celebrated by splashing water on friends, relatives, and even strangers to symbolize the washing away of the old and welcoming the new. The streets of Bangkok, Chiang Mai, and other Thai cities come alive with water battles and jubilant festivities.

The vibrant Mardi Gras festival in New Orleans is famous for its colorful parades, spectacular costumes, and celebratory atmosphere. This celebration, held every year on Fat Tuesday, has strong roots in the city's history and culture. Travolex's
