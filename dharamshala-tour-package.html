  • Day 1

    Arrival in Dharamshala – Settling into the Himalayas

    Your Dharamshala journey begins as you arrive at Gaggal Airport or via a scenic drive from Pathankot/Delhi. Upon arrival, a Travolex representative will assist with your hotel transfer. After check-in, spend some time relaxing amidst the cool pine-scented air and majestic Dhauladhar range views. Later in the evening, head out for a gentle walk in the peaceful town, visiting the St. John in the Wilderness Church, a neo-Gothic structure nestled in the deodar forest. The day ends with a quiet dinner and an overnight stay at your hotel in Dharamshala.

  • Day 2

    Exploring McLeod Ganj – The Tibetan Heart of India

    After breakfast, head to McLeod Ganj, the cultural and spiritual heart of Dharamshala. Begin with a visit to the Tsuglagkhang Complex, the official residence of His Holiness the Dalai Lama, and explore the serene Namgyal Monastery and Tibetan Museum. From there, take a walk through the bustling local market filled with Tibetan handicrafts, cafes, and bookstores. In the afternoon, visit the cascading Bhagsunag Waterfall and the nearby Bhagsunath Temple, a site sacred to Hindus and revered by the Gorkha community. If time permits, enjoy tea with a view at one of the many hillside cafés. Return to your hotel in Dharamshala for the night.

  • Day 3

    Naddi Village – Dal Lake – Sunset Views

    Start your day with a drive to Naddi Village, a quiet Himalayan hamlet offering spectacular views of the snow-capped peaks. From Naddi, enjoy a short nature walk through forested paths to Dal Lake, a small but sacred lake surrounded by tall deodar trees. You may also visit the Galu Devi Temple en route. The area is perfect for meditation or a peaceful break from city life. Later, head to Sunset Point near Naddi to witness a golden horizon over the Kangra Valley — a photographer's delight. Return to Dharamshala for a relaxed evening and overnight stay.

  • Day 4

    Day Trip to Kangra – History & Spirituality

    After breakfast, take a day trip to Kangra, located around 25 kilometers from Dharamshala. Begin with a visit to the Kangra Fort, one of India’s oldest forts, offering panoramic views and stories from centuries of rule. Continue to the Mata Brajeshwari Temple, a revered Shakti Peeth, followed by a visit to the Norbulingka Institute, a stunning center preserving traditional Tibetan art, craftsmanship, and culture. Walk through its lush gardens, watch artisans at work, and even pick up handcrafted souvenirs. After this enriching cultural excursion, return to Dharamshala by evening for your final overnight stay.

  • Day 5

    Departure from Dharamshala

    After a leisurely breakfast, check out from your hotel. Depending on your departure time, you may squeeze in a final walk or café visit before heading to Gaggal Airport or your onward destination. As you leave Dharamshala, you carry with you memories of spiritual calm, mountain magic, and the warm simplicity of Tibetan and Himachali culture.
