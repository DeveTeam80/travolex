  • Day 1

    Arrival in Paro – Transfer to Thimphu

    Upon arrival at Paro International Airport, one of the most scenic airports in the world, a Travolex representative will meet and assist you. After a warm welcome, begin your journey with a drive to Thimphu, the capital city of Bhutan. The scenic drive along the Paro and Thimphu rivers takes about 1.5 hours. After check-in at your hotel in Thimphu, enjoy a relaxed evening stroll along Norzin Lam, where you’ll get a first glimpse of Bhutan’s calm, traditional lifestyle. Dinner will be served at the hotel or a local Bhutanese restaurant.

  • Day 2

    Thimphu Sightseeing

    Start your day with a visit to the National Memorial Chorten, built in honor of Bhutan’s third king. Continue to the Buddha Dordenma, a massive statue overlooking Thimphu Valley, followed by a visit to the Changangkha Lhakhang, one of the oldest temples in the region. After lunch, explore the Folk Heritage Museum, the National Textile Museum, and Tashichho Dzong, the seat of Bhutan’s government and monastic body. In the evening, you may choose to visit a local market or enjoy a traditional Bhutanese cultural performance.
  • Day 3

    Thimphu to Punakha via Dochula Pass

    After breakfast, drive to Punakha, the ancient capital of Bhutan, via the spectacular Dochula Pass at 3,100 meters. Weather permitting, you’ll enjoy panoramic views of the eastern Himalayas. Stop at the 108 Druk Wangyal Chortens for photos and spiritual reflection. Upon arrival in Punakha, visit the majestic Punakha Dzong, considered the most beautiful in Bhutan, nestled between two rivers. Later, take a short hike to Chimi Lhakhang, the fertility temple dedicated to the Divine Madman, and learn about Bhutanese spiritual traditions. Enjoy the tranquil rural landscapes before settling into your hotel for the night.
  • Day 4

    Punakha to Paro – Leisure and Exploration

    After breakfast, begin your return drive to Paro, stopping for tea or photos en route. Upon arrival, check into your hotel and enjoy a relaxed afternoon. Visit Rinpung Dzong, which offers beautiful views and insight into Bhutanese monastic life. If time allows, take a walk through Paro town, where traditional architecture houses handicraft shops, cafés, and bakeries. In the evening, consider a traditional hot stone bath experience, a rejuvenating local wellness ritual.
  • Day 5

    Paro – Hike to Tiger’s Nest Monastery

    Today is the highlight of your Bhutan trip—a hike to the legendary Taktsang Monastery, also known as Tiger’s Nest. Perched dramatically on a cliffside at 3,120 meters, this sacred site is where Guru Padmasambhava is said to have meditated after flying in on a tigress. The hike takes about 5–6 hours round trip and rewards you with spiritual energy, panoramic views, and profound silence. After descending, enjoy lunch at a local restaurant and spend your evening reflecting on your journey. Dinner is served at your hotel or at a traditional Bhutanese farmhouse, offering an authentic dining experience.
  • Day 6

    Departure from Paro

    After breakfast, check out from your hotel and transfer to Paro International Airport for your departure. As your journey ends, you leave with lasting memories of Bhutan’s peaceful landscapes, deep spirituality, and warm hospitality.
